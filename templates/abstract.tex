\chapter{Abstract}

{
    \color{orange}
    An abstract is a condensation of the content into one paragraph, at most one page.
    The abstract should stand on its own and present the motivation, methods, goals, and results of the work.
    A good abstract should be formulated very consciously, because crucial key points of the work must become clear.

    \begin{description}
        \item[Context:]{Get the reader on board with a short introduction using one or two sentences.}
        \item[State of research:]{Summarize the current state of research very briefly. Address shortcomings of previous results to pave the way towards your own research question.}
        \item[Research gap:]{Formulate your research question explaining what is missing so far or which aspects need to be examined more closely.}
        \item[Own approach:]{Describe the methods you employ to solve the research question.}
        \item[Results:]{Summarize your results and their implications.}
    \end{description}
}
