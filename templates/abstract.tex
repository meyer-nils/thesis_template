\chapter{Abstract}

\textcolor{orange}{
An abstract is a condensation of the content into one paragraph, at most one page.
The abstract should stand on its own and present the motivation, methods, goals, and results of the work to someone who does not have time to read the entire work.
It must enable a reader to decide whether he should read this work. The abstract is of great importance and should be formulated very consciously, because crucial key points of the work must become clear.
\begin{description}
    \item[Context:]{First, the reader should get on board. For this purpose, one or two sentences are used to introduce the subject.}
    \item[State of research:]{At this point, the current state of research is summarized very briefly. In particular, shortcomings of previous results should be addressed in order to work towards one's own research question. }
    \item[Research gap:]{Here the own research question is worked out. What is missing so far or which aspects need to be examined more closely?}
    \item[Own approach:]{This section describes the methods used - what is one's approach to solving the research question?}
    \item[Results:]{What came out of the work and which conclusions may be drawn?}
\end{description}
You may also summarize your work in a \href{https://www.elsevier.com/authors/tools-and-resources/visual-abstract}{graphical abstract} and use it as the title image of this thesis. 
}
