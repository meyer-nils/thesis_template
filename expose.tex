	\section*{\EnglishTitle}
    {\color{orange}
    	The first paragraph deals with the problem of the thesis. What is the motivation to deal with the topic and how can the general public benefit from the research project? 
    	
    	The second paragraph briefly describes the current state of research. What preliminary work is available and what are the relevant publications in the field? References may be added with \verb|\cite{key}|, e.g. a reference to Einstein \cite{Einstein1905}.
    	
    	The third paragraph elaborates the knowledge gap. What is the area that has not yet been addressed by the state of research and offers potential for improvement?
    	
    	The fourth paragraph formulates a research question that arises from the knowledge gap and is to be investigated in the thesis. It also defines the objective or a hypothesis to be investigated.
    	
    	The fifth paragraph describes methods and material to be used to address the question. The procedure can then be supplemented by a rough time schedule:
    	
    	\begin{itemize}
    		\item First step
    		\item Second step
    		\item Third step 
            \item \dots
    	\end{itemize}
